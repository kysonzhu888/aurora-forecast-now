Local knowledge

Where to watch the southern lights near Hobart

Hobart is the southern hemisphere's most accessible aurora city: nowhere else at 43 degrees south combines an airport, sealed roads, and open Southern Ocean horizons this close to the auroral zone.

Viewing spot

Goat Bluff lookout

On the South Arm Peninsula about 40 minutes from town, the local photographers' benchmark with open sea to the south.

Viewing spot

Clifton Beach

A dark southern beach slightly closer in, with dune foregrounds.

Viewing spot

Bruny Island

For committed nights: ferry access and some of Tasmania's darkest coastal sky.

Season & timing

When to go

Aurora australis is possible year-round, but Tasmanian winter, May to August, offers the longest dark windows; equinox weeks often carry the strongest geomagnetic activity.

Photography

Camera notes

Face due south over water and expose for the horizon glow: Tasmanian displays often read as color beams on camera well before the naked eye catches them.

FAQ

Hobart aurora questions

Can you see the southern lights from Hobart city?

During strong storms, yes, but light pollution mutes it. The South Arm Peninsula, 30 to 45 minutes away, is dramatically better and is where locals go when alerts fire.

What Kp do I need in Hobart?

Cameras pick up displays from about Kp 4 to 5; memorable naked-eye shows usually need Kp 6 or more with clear, dark southern horizons.

Can I see the southern lights in Hobart tonight?
